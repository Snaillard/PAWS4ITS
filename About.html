ABOUT PAWS


We are a volunteer-based animal welfare NGO that educates and spreads information about animal care, control, and welfare, and actively campaigns against animal cruelty and neglect, including dog and horse fights and the use of wild animals for entertainment. PAWS runs an animal shelter for over 300 cats and dogs rescued from cruelty or neglect, where they are rehabilitated and given a second chance at a good life. We also have a low-cost clinic that provides veterinary services to the indigent community, including spay and neuter.



OUR STORY


The Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS) was founded in 1954 by a British educator named Muriel Jay. The organization's original purpose was to raise funds by making and selling stuffed toys and other items, and it also had a clinic to treat injured animals and bicycle patrols to pick up stray animals. PAWS became less active after Muriel Jay left the Philippines, but was reorganized in 1986 by Nita Hontiveros-Lichauco. In 1996, a new group of members joined PAWS with more aggressive ideas. They set up a cattery for rescued cats and sent rescued dogs to a farm in Pangasinan province. In 2000, construction began on the Paws Animal Rehabilitation Center (PARC), which is currently a temporary refuge for animals rescued from cruelty and neglect. Today, PAWS is run by its directors Anna Cabrera, Cristina Martinez, Rich Illustre, and Doris Cruz.
